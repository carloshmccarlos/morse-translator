## Design Context

### Users
People using the app to convert speech or text into Morse code, usually in a focused utility context: students, radio hobbyists, developers, educators, and puzzle builders.

### Brand Personality
Technical, precise, experimental, and terse. The interface should feel like a compact signal console, not a marketing page.

### Aesthetic Direction
Dark console-inspired UI with amber and emerald signal accents, high contrast surfaces, compact panels, and minimal explanatory copy. The page should read as a functional tool first.

### Design Principles
- Keep the main task visible immediately.
- Preserve a clear heading hierarchy.
- Use concise labels and short helper text.
- Favor strong contrast and tight spacing over decorative noise.
- Make status, playback, and translation states obvious at a glance.
